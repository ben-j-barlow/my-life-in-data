# Yann LeCun - Lex Fridman

- LLMs train on 2^13 bytes but a 4 year old has seen 2^15 bytes, similar amount of bandwidth for touch and a little less for audio, so relatively 2^13 is not that much information
- Amount of computation per token constant, unlike humans
- There is an abstraction above language that humans plan their sentences over before implementing their idea in a language; an autoregressive LLMs can’t perform such planning
- Hard for videos: language is discrete so a probability distribution over words is feasible, but distributions over high dimensional. continuous spaces would be necessary for videos which is very difficult, and has prevented researchers from making significant progress in video prediction
- Autoregressife LLMs can’t do 4 fundamental human traits and therefore cannot permit super intelligent life: the capacity to understand the physical world, the ability to remember and retrieve things, the ability to reason, and the ability to plan
- Thinking fast and slow: System 1 & 2. AR LLMs can’t do system 2.
- AI transition comparable to printing press. Books were cheaper. More people learned to read. Made everyone smarter and transformed the world. Bad effects: 200 years of religious conflicts in Europe because everyone read the bible and disagreed things. Overall, net effect was good.
